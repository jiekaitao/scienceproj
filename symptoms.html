Detecting And Self-Diagnosing Symptoms

Often, a patient will not notice they have papillary carcinoma because they are not really any symptoms. You might only find out you have it if you have an imaging test for another issue or during a physical check-up your doctor feels a lump (nodule) on your thyroid.

Affect on health

Papillary Carcinoma, among all other cancers, destroys healthy cells by refusing to undergo apoptosis. The body tries to react; its defence, however, is impaired by its own cells' offensive and damaging functions.



What started as a minor annoyance

As the cancer progressed through the body, the immune system fights back even harder, which contributes to the problem. As cells die, the victim experiences persistent cough, blood-tinged saliva, blood in stool, amenia, and mant other stool variations.
