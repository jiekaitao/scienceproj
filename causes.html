Causes

No one is sure of what causes papillary carcinoma, but there are certain things that can increase its likelihood. The mutations of the RET/PTC gene are apparent in 10% to 30% of patients diagnosed with papillary carcinoma and is most evident in children who have been exposed to radiation. Mutations of the BRAF gene also appear in 30% to 70% of patients diagnosed and cause the body to produce more aggressive cancer that metalizes(spread to other parts of the body). The BRAF gene mutation is less frequent in children who have been exposed to radiation.
