General Information

Papillary carcinomas are a slow growing growing type of cancer and they account for 80% of diagnosed cases of thyroid cancer. They are a type of differentiated cancer, meaning that they are composed from cells that are mature and look like cells. Differentiated cancer is less dangerous than undifferentiated cancer, composed of immature cells. Papillary carcinoma can develop in both lobes of the thyroid and can spread to the lymph nodes. Luckily for the patients, papillary is normally a very slow spreading cancer that has a very high mortality rate, over 98% of patients survive. The thyroid gland is what's responsible for controlling your metabolism.

Cancer Cells (Papillary Thyroid)

Representing more than 75 percent of thyroid cancer cases, Papillary Thyroid cancer cells (apppearing as violet/pink/purple) are the most common thyoid cancer.
