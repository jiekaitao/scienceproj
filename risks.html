Risk Factors


    • * A genetic history of goiters, or the abnormal enlargement of the thyroid.

    • * A genetic history of thryoid cancer.

    • * General exposure to high levels of radiation.

    • * Being between 25 and 65 years old, female, and Asian










In very rare cases, papillary carcinoma can run in the family. Certain genetic disorders like familial adenomatous polyposis (FAP), Gardner syndrome, and Cowden disease can increase your chances of getting papillary carcinoma. Some say that radiation therapy as a child can increase one's likelihood. It is also noted that low iodine can also increase one’s risk of being diagnosed. Papillary carcinoma is more common in women than compared to men, almost 3 times more frequently. The age risk for getting papillary carcinoma also differ in men and women. For women the years of highest risk is in their 40s and 50s and for men it is in their 60s and 70s.
